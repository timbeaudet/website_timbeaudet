This post has been on the ole back-burner for a while, since I was busy with October Challenge and Artember. Actually it was a graphics feature I wrote and added to TurtleBrains during September since someone was requesting it as a future feature and I had a good use case; tv screens at the track showing the race. (Also car mirror would be another good use case).

[cpp]  
//Initialize the RenderTarget object, probably member variable of your scene.  
tbGraphics::RenderTarget mRenderTarget;

//When ready to draw on the&nbsp;target, bind it, then unbind it when finished.  
mRenderTarget.BindRenderTarget();  
RenderOnTvTarget(); //Setup and render the car.  
mRenderTarget.UnbindRenderTarget();

//Finally bind the target\&#8217;s texture when ready&nbsp;to use the target on an object.  
tbGraphics::theTextureManager.BindTexture(mRenderTarget.GetTextureHandle());  
[/cpp]

That is all there is to it. This featureÂ is supported on Windows, Mac OS X and Linux builds of TurtleBrains.
